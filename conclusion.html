    • 5 Conclusion

    In this analysis, three bioinformatics tools for the discovery of RiPP were evaluated, namely, MetaMiner, NeuRipp and FBGAN. Publicly available sequence data from the NCBI and antiSMASH databases were extracted and analysed using these tools. A wide variety of bioinformatics programmes, including AntiSMASH, Prodigal and HMMER packages, were also used for the pre-processing and analysis of the data. Results of the analysis highlight the limitations of each RiPP discovery tool and led to the discussion of the integration of currently available tools and future modifications that would allow a more comprehensive study of RiPPs. To conclude, currently available RiPP discovery tools are still limited by either our current knowledge about the biosynthetic processes of RiPP or the number of empirically validated precursor peptides. However, they have laid a solid foundation for the future development of exhaustive tools that would facilitate the identification of RiPPs in a high throughput manner or the generation of de novo RiPP sequences.
